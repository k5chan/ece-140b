Design principles

What basic information does a typical user need to have access to on first interaction with your product?

The user needs to know that the sensors need to be placed at joints along the body. The user also needs to know how to connect the app to the device in addition to knowing how to navigate the app.

What IA Design Principles (Dan Brown) are important for your product?

I think all of the IA Design Principles are important for my product but I think that the principle of growth and disclosure are the most important for my product. The principle of disclosure is really important because our product will have multiple layers of complexity and it would be best if we progressively disclose information rather than have an information dump on the user. The principle of growth is also important because our product is in its beta form so it would be best if we gave our product IA room to grow and develop.

Which structural Organizational Principle(s) make(s) the most the sense for your product?

I think that the Sequential and the Chronological IA Organizational Principle make the most sense for my product. The Sequential Organizational Principle makes sense because our product will have to include alot of user input so we will need to implement a navigational process. The Chronological Organizational Principle makes sense for some of our product because we want the user to look at past stats of his/her self and compare. This is because our product in a way is a performance tracker.
